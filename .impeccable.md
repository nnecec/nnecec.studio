## Design Context

### Users

Primary users are technical readers looking for React, JavaScript, performance, and frontend
architecture writing. Secondary users are visitors judging the author's taste, technical maturity,
and authorship through the design of the archive itself.

### Brand Personality

Sharp, authored, editorial. The interface should feel opinionated and precise rather than neutral,
friendly, or utility-heavy.

### Aesthetic Direction

Use a warm-neutral editorial base with stronger contrast blocks, asymmetric publication-style
composition, and designed metadata rails. The archive should feel closer to a technical periodical
than a blog theme. Avoid SaaS dashboard patterns, generic blog-card repetition, and layouts that
optimize only for reading.

### Design Principles

1. Design-led composition comes before generic reading templates.
2. Archive pages should feel curated, with a clear lead story and supporting ledger structure.
3. Metadata should be integrated into the layout as editorial framing, not added as form controls or
   chip clutter.
4. Detail pages should stage the opening, then settle into a calmer long-form reading field.
5. Mobile layouts must preserve the same authored hierarchy instead of collapsing into a default
   stacked blog page.
